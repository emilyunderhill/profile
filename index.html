Emily Underhill

Engineer to Conservationist to Programmer!

After volunteering in South Africa and Costa Rica on conservation projects I am now training in programming to help non-profit organisations spread awareness for their cause.

Where it began

Starting off as a mechanical engineering graduate all I knew is I want to do something exciting with my new degree, and what's more exciting than designing cars right?? Wrong. There's nothing worse than sitting behind a computer looking at one bolt all day. On top of that cars are one of the main reasons for our planet's ultimate demise, doesn't quite match my ethical compass. After many existential crises and pondering the future of our world I decided to quit my "dream job" and take a break travelling.

South Africa

I found myself travelling to South Africa for 3 months to volunteer on game reserves. An incredible experience, surrounded by like minded people and engulfed in nature every day. I'll have to admit it wasn't the most scientific conservation experiences, but it ignited a feeling that is hard to explain. Waking up to elephants everyday or the sound of a distant lion's roar... There's nothing quite like it. We learnt how to track wildlife and document key species, while taking to a machete to remove invasive fauna.
